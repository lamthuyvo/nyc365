Rocky beginnings

New beginnings are always harder than we think. At first, they seem exciting and full of possibilities but often we hit a few bumps.

Stephanie faced a number of life changes when she moved to New York from San Francisco in 2014. As someone who works with audio, she found solace in music. This is the story of her first year in New York as told through her most played songs.

The dots surrounding this text represent all the songs Stephanie listened to 10 times or more during her first year in New York. There are also a handful of songs she found important and weren't played as much.

Blue dots ● represent songs that she associated with sadness, yellow dots ● are songs that she listened to with mixed feelings and magenta-colored dots ● are songs that made her happy.

In some ways, music was a way to deal with the inevitable loneliness that she sometimes felt in the city. The x-axis represents the number of days passed since she arrived in New York, the y-axis shows time of day. Do you see how she slowly learns to love her new home?

What made this transition harder than anything was a breakup. Her boyfriend, who moved with her to New York from the West Coast, broke up with her halfway through her first year. These are the songs she listened to during her breakup to deal with her emotions.

Heartbreak dance party!

What followed was a musical exercise in self-healing. These are all the songs she associated with her breakup. Let's see what music helped Stephanie through her breakup.

Put on your headphones!

Stephanie listened to songs like Ariana Grande's 'Break Your Heart Right Back' when she was feeling low.

La Roux's Bulletproof was one of many songs that Stephanie listened to when she felt anxious.

She found courage and happiness through songs like Jhameel's Feisty . She said this song helped her get pumped when she started dating again.
